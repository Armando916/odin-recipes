Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a hearty and delicious Italian dish made by layering sheets of pasta with a rich tomato-based meat or vegetable sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and generous amounts of melted mozzarella and Parmesan. This baked casserole is known for its comforting flavors, combining the tanginess of the sauce with the creaminess of the cheese and the satisfying texture of the pasta. Often seasoned with garlic, herbs like basil and oregano, and sometimes red wine, lasagna is a versatile meal that can be adapted to various tastes, including vegetarian or gluten-free options. It’s a timeless favorite perfect for family gatherings or special occasions.

Ingredients

  • pasta
  • cheese
  • sauce
  • meat or vegetables

Steps

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Brown ground beef or sauté vegetables, then add marinara sauce and let it simmer.
  2. Make the Cheese Mixture: In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, an egg, grated Parmesan, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Layer the Lasagna: In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of sauce, followed by a layer of noodles. Add a layer of the cheese mixture, then more sauce, and repeat until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of sauce and shredded mozzarella.
  4. Bake and Serve: Cover with foil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for about 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly. Let it cool slightly before serving. Enjoy your homemade lasagna!
